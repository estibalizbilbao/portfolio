Resfeber – Making Solo Travel Safer and More Accessible

  1. Home
  2. Resfeber

Traveling alone is an experience full of possibilities, but also challenges. While more people are choosing to explore the world solo, many still hesitate—especially women—due to concerns about safety, organization, and social connection. Resfeber is a conceptual travel app designed to address these concerns, making solo travel not just possible, but more intuitive, secure, and enjoyable.




Designing for Real Needs

This project started with a deep dive into the realities of solo travelers, especially women aged 20-35. Through interviews, surveys, and research, I identified key barriers that prevent people from traveling alone:

  • Fear of insecurity – Many potential solo travelers worry about safety in unknown places.
  • Lack of organization – Managing travel documents, reservations, and logistics can be overwhelming.
  • Social barriers – The fear of loneliness and difficulty connecting with locals or other travelers.

The goal was not just to design a tool but to rethink the solo travel experience from the ground up, using user-centered design methodologies to create a meaningful solution.


A User-Centered Design Journey

My approach was firmly rooted in User-Centered Design (UCD). I started by conducting interviews, surveys, and desk research to understand travelers’ concerns firsthand. From there, I mapped out their experiences using experience maps and explored ideas through brainstorming sessions and forced associations.

By integrating users’ real challenges into the design process, I ensured that every feature in Resfeber was built around actual needs—from intuitive organization tools to safety-focused features. The project evolved through multiple iterations, always prioritizing usability and emotional reassurance.


The Solution – A Travel Companion in Your Pocket

Resfeber is designed to act as a digital travel assistant, helping solo travelers stay organized and feel more secure while on the move. The app concept includes:

  • Centralized travel management – A place to store and organize all travel documents, reservations, and emergency contacts.
  • Safety-focused features – Quick access to local emergency numbers, maps, and real-time safety updates.
  • Cultural insights – Guidance on local customs, social norms, and travel restrictions.
  • Social connection tools – Recommendations on safe ways to meet locals and other travelers.

More Than an App, A Mindset Shift

Resfeber is about more than just convenience; it’s about giving people the confidence to explore the world on their own terms. By addressing both practical concerns and emotional barriers, this project aims to make solo travel a more inclusive and empowering experience.
