Savra – Smarter Food Management for a Sustainable Future

  1. Home
  2. Savra

Food waste is a major global issue, impacting both the environment and household budgets. Savra was created to address this challenge by making food management effortless through intelligent automation. Instead of requiring people to change their habits, it integrates seamlessly into their daily routines, helping them optimize grocery shopping, track expiration dates, and reduce waste.


Understanding the Problem – A Research-Driven Approach

To design a meaningful solution, we first needed to understand the real challenges people face when managing food at home. The research phase included:

  • Desk Research to explore sustainability trends and food waste reports.
  • User Interviews with consumers, chefs, and sustainability experts.
  • Surveys to gather quantitative insights on grocery shopping and waste habits.
  • Benchmarking to analyze existing solutions and identify gaps in the market.

This phase revealed three key pain points:

  1. Lack of awareness – People don’t always realize how much food they waste.
  2. Difficulty in planning – Grocery shopping is often unstructured, leading to overbuying.
  3. Inconvenience in tracking food expiration – Managing expiration dates is time-consuming.


From Insights to Action – The Design Process

Savra was developed using Design Thinking, Service Design, and Business Innovation methodologies, ensuring the solution was desirable, feasible, and viable.

The design process included:

  • Experience mapping to define user pain points and needs.
  • Co-creation workshops to ideate solutions based on real user behaviors.
  • Service Blueprinting to design a seamless user experience.

This approach led to Savra’s core functionalities:


  • An AI-powered smart grocery list that updates automatically, preventing unnecessary purchases.
  • Expiration tracking with reminders to help users consume food before it goes to waste.
  • Integration with supermarkets to centralize shopping data and optimize purchasing.
  • Impact tracking metrics to visualize money and food saved over time.



Prototyping and Validation

A high-fidelity prototype was developed in Figma and tested with potential users. The validation phase confirmed that Savra was intuitive, effective, and easy to integrate into everyday life. User feedback led to refinements in the onboarding process, notification system, and interface design, ensuring a seamless and non-disruptive experience.

What is Savra?

Savra is an intelligent food management assistant that helps users reduce waste, save money, and shop more efficiently—without extra effort.

It works by:

  • Automatically generating a shopping list based on what the user already has at home.
  • Tracking expiration dates and notifying users before food goes to waste.
  • Syncing with supermarkets to keep an updated inventory of purchased items.
  • Suggesting recipes based on soon-to-expire ingredients, helping users make the most of what they already have before it goes to waste.
  • Providing insights on food and cost savings, empowering users to make better choices over time.

By integrating into existing routines rather than requiring behavior change, Savra makes sustainable food consumption simple, practical, and intuitive.
